# Quotation & Document Submission Workflow Guide (Malaysia)
## Reference Document — SettleOps Quotation MCP Tool

---

## 1. Document Submission Process

The car owner must submit the following documents to the **Panel Workshop**:

| Step | Document | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Police Report** | Official record of the accident — must be lodged within 24 hours |
| 2 | **Keputusan Case** | Police investigation result — determines fault/liability |
| 3 | **Policy** | Full insurance policy document |
| 4 | **Cover Note** | Summary of coverage: sum insured, excess, endorsements, cover type |

### Missing Documents
If any documents are missing:
- The Panel Workshop obtains them from the **Insurance Agent**, or
- Requests them directly from the **car owner**
- The claim process cannot proceed to insurer approval without all 4 documents

---

## 2. Offer Letter & Quotation Process

After receiving the documents, the Panel Workshop:

1. **Inspects the vehicle** — photographs damage, identifies damaged parts
2. **Prepares a repair quotation** — itemised list of parts, labour, paint, towing
3. **Creates an Offer Letter** — formal document containing:
   - Vehicle details (make, model, plate number, year)
   - Itemised repair costs with part numbers where applicable
   - Total repair estimate
   - Estimated repair duration
4. **Submits to the insurance company**:
   - The Offer Letter
   - All 4 required documents from Section 1
   - Supporting photographs of the damage

---

## 3. Insurance Company Approval Process

Upon receiving the submission:

1. **Review the quotation** — check if repair costs are reasonable against market benchmarks
2. **Verify documentation** — ensure all required documents are present and valid
3. **Check policy terms** — confirm coverage type, excess, sum insured, and endorsements
4. **Decision:**
   - If quotation is **reasonable**: approve, **sign and stamp** the Offer Letter
   - If quotation is **unreasonable**: request revision from the workshop
   - If damage is **extensive** (see Section 4): assign an adjuster

---

## 4. Adjuster Assignment Rules

An **Adjuster** is appointed by the insurance company when:

### 4.1 Primary Trigger — Damage Exceeds 40% of Sum Insured
- When the verified repair total exceeds **40% of the policy's sum insured (policy cap)**
- This is the most commonly used threshold across Malaysian insurers
- Example: Policy cap is MYR 50,000 → Adjuster is assigned if repair exceeds MYR 20,000

### 4.2 Secondary Trigger — Price Threshold
- Some insurers set a fixed MYR threshold (e.g., repair > MYR 15,000)
- This is insurer-specific and may be stated in internal guidelines

### 4.3 Adjuster Responsibilities
The adjuster is responsible for:
- **Verifying the quotation** provided by the panel workshop
- **Inspecting the vehicle** to confirm damage matches the quotation
- **Checking repair items** — ensuring each quoted item corresponds to actual damage
- **Identifying pre-existing damage** not related to the reported incident
- **Submitting a report** to the insurer with findings before approval

### 4.4 Adjuster Scope Limitations
The adjuster does **NOT**:
- Handle documents such as the police report
- Determine fault or liability (that is the Keputusan Case)
- Make approval decisions (that is the insurer's role)
- Interact directly with the car owner (they work through the panel workshop)

---

## 5. Quotation Validation Checklist

When auditing a workshop quotation, verify:

| Check | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| **Parts match damage** | Every quoted part should correspond to visible damage in photos |
| **No phantom items** | No parts quoted that show no evidence of damage |
| **Price within benchmarks** | Each part price within OEM/Standard range (±20%) |
| **Labour rates reasonable** | Labour hours proportional to number of panels/parts |
| **Paint scope correct** | Paint/refinish only for damaged panels, not full vehicle |
| **Towing justified** | Only if vehicle was immobilised at scene (max MYR 400) |
| **Betterment applied** | If vehicle ≥ 5 years, note betterment deduction in payout |
| **Total vs policy cap** | Flag if total exceeds 40% of sum insured (adjuster needed) |
| **Repair vs replace** | Minor damage should be repaired, not replaced |
